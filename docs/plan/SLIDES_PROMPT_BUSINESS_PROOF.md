# Prompt for Creating Startup Slides (Business Proof)

Use this prompt in your slide-generation tool.

## Master Prompt
Create a 5-slide startup pitch deck for **ContextSuite**.

Primary goal: **Prove this can become a real business**, not just a technical demo.

Pitch format context:
- 2 minutes pitch
- 2 minutes demo
- 2 minutes Q&A

Tone and style:
- Investor-minded, clear, specific, and concise.
- No buzzword fluff.
- Use concrete ICPs, realistic assumptions, and believable numbers.
- Prefer short bullets with strong headlines.
- Each slide should be readable in 20-25 seconds.

Design direction:
- Clean, modern B2B SaaS look.
- High-contrast typography and simple visual hierarchy.
- Use one key visual per slide max.
- Keep consistency in color, spacing, and icon style.

Business context to include:
- Product: ContextSuite (AI governance and memory layer for coding assistants).
- Problem: teams reintroduce old bugs because implementation rationale and constraints are lost across sessions.
- Solution: Context Agent reviews plans before execution, checks historical constraints, supports approval routing, and reduces repeated regressions.
- Pricing baseline:
  - Individual: $15/month for up to 5 repos/projects.
  - Starter: ~$10/seat/month with team tiers and discounts at scale.
  - Add-ons: Slack, Telegram, and integration plugins.

Now generate these exact slides:

### Slide 1 - Market Problem
Include:
- Exactly who has this problem (specific ICP, not generic "businesses").
- Market size framing (# teams/companies/users and spend context).
- Current alternatives and why they fail.
- One strong takeaway line: investors fund markets, not features.

### Slide 2 - Your Solution
Include:
- What ContextSuite is in one sentence.
- Why this approach is 10x better than alternatives.
- Keep this slide short and very clear.

### Slide 3 - Business Model
Include:
- Who pays (buyer and economic user).
- Pricing model and tiers.
- Unit economics example with revenue vs cost-to-serve and margin intuition.
- Show real understanding of how this can make money.

### Slide 4 - Go-to-Market Plan
Include:
- Specific plan to get first 10 customers.
- Distribution advantage (community, integrations, partner channels, content, outbound).
- Why teams would switch from current workflow.
- Avoid vague tactics like "post on social media."

### Slide 5 - Why You / Why Now
Include:
- Why this team is credible to build it.
- Why this timing matters now.
- Whether the team would go full-time if traction is validated.
- End with a conviction line.

Output requirements:
- Provide slide-by-slide content with:
  - Slide title
  - 3-6 bullets
  - Optional speaker note (1-2 lines)
- Keep all claims plausible for a hackathon-stage startup.
- Do not invent fake customer logos or fake revenue.
